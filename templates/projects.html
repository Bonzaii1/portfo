SMS System Fiji/PNG

One of my significant projects involved the design and implementation of a student management system tailored for universities in Fiji and Papua New Guinea. This venture allowed me to utilize my skills in Java, SpringBoot, and Oracle Databases, which not only enhanced my proficiency in developing robust systems but also honed my expertise in object-oriented programming, data structures, and algorithms. This Project was a lot of fun and had many different challenges, one of them being modifying the Databases to reflect the information that they use in their respective universities. Removing a lot of the current fields that they use had some heavy consequences on the system, as it was reliant on many of them. So it was our job to go in and patch in the holes that the information would have left behind.

UMScan

Using computer vision tricks, our system would keep an eagle eye on cars coming in. It's like having Sherlock Holmes in the form of software, figuring out where the license plates are, and even reading the secret code written on them. How? We call it OCR, or Optical Character Recognition, which is a fancy way of saying our system can read those letters and numbers on the plates. But here's the real magic: our system won't just say "Hi" to anyone. It's got a list â€“ a list of cars that are allowed to party inside our campus. When a car rolls in, UMScan checks its name against t he list. If it's an invited guest (meaning it's on the list), the gates open and the party begins. If not, well, the system won't budge. Unauthorized entries? Not on its watch! Plus, it keeps a journal of all the cars that come and go. Security? Check! Efficiency? Double-check!

Perimetral Security with Raspberry Pi

Imagine a datacenter shielded by an invisible fortress. That's exactly what we've built with our "Enhanced Perimeter Security" project. We turned a regular datacenter into a fortress of digital sensors, all thanks to our trusty Raspberry Pi. Our secret weapon? The concept of threading. Each sensor, from motion detectors to temperature gauges, runs in its own thread. They're like sentinels on the lookout, working together seamlessly yet independently to capture real-time metrics. This multi-threaded approach ensures that no anomaly slips through the cracks, allowing us to monitor the perimeter with unparalleled precision. But that's just the beginning. We've also established a Mosquitto broker system, which serves as the nerve center. It coordinates all these vigilant sensors and relays critical data to an API that we've custom-built. If any sensor detects an outlier, you can bet our system will catch it, flag it, and swiftly notify the right people through our alert API. Not only that but the same broker also sends a message through the OneSignal service, allowing these same alerts to run straight to your phone.
